# User Guide for Charity Staff

A practical guide for staff, volunteers, and helpline operators using the Resource Classification System.

---

## Welcome

This system helps you find the right resources for the people you support—quickly and confidently. Whether you're on the helpline, responding to emails, or preparing resource packs, this guide will help you make the most of the classification system.

---

## What Does This System Do?

Think of it as a **super-smart filing system** that:

✅ **Organizes 4,000+ resources** with detailed tags  
✅ **Finds exactly what you need** in seconds  
✅ **Suggests when to use each resource** with staff guidance  
✅ **Shows confidence scores** so you know how reliable the match is  
✅ **Learns and improves** over time  

---

## Understanding Tags

Every resource is tagged with information about:

### 1. Who It's For (Personas)

- **persona:patient** - The person with encephalitis
- **persona:caregiver** - Family members, friends, carers
- **persona:parent** - Parents of children with encephalitis
- **persona:professional** - Healthcare professionals, researchers
- **persona:bereaved** - Those who have lost someone

**Tip**: Many resources work for multiple personas.

### 2. When to Use It (Journey Stages)

- **stage:pre_diagnosis** - Before diagnosis, seeking help, symptoms
- **stage:acute_hospital** - Hospital admission, acute treatment
- **stage:early_recovery** - First 6-12 months after hospital
- **stage:long_term_management** - Living with long-term effects (12+ months)

**Tip**: Match resources to where the person is in their journey.

### 3. What They're Experiencing (Symptoms)

- **symptom:memory** - Memory problems, cognitive issues
- **symptom:behaviour** - Behavioral changes, personality changes
- **symptom:seizures** - Seizures, epilepsy
- **symptom:fatigue** - Fatigue, exhaustion
- **symptom:mobility** - Movement, coordination issues
- **symptom:speech** - Speech, communication difficulties
- **symptom:emotional** - Anxiety, depression, emotional regulation
- **symptom:sleep** - Sleep disturbances
- **symptom:sensory** - Sensory issues, hallucinations
- **symptom:pain** - Headaches, pain management

**Tip**: People often have multiple symptoms. Search for the most pressing one first.

### 4. What They Need to Know (Topics)

- **topic:diagnosis** - Diagnostic procedures, tests
- **topic:treatment** - Treatment options, medications, therapies
- **topic:research** - Research studies, clinical trials
- **topic:memory** - Memory-specific content
- **topic:behaviour** - Behavior and personality changes
- **topic:school** - Education, returning to school
- **topic:work** - Employment, returning to work
- **topic:legal** - Legal matters, benefits, rights
- **topic:travel** - Travel advice, vaccinations
- **topic:rehabilitation** - Rehabilitation, therapy
- **topic:prevention** - Prevention, vaccines, awareness

**Tip**: Start with immediate needs (diagnosis, treatment) before long-term topics.

### 5. Specific Conditions

- **type:autoimmune** - Autoimmune encephalitis
- **type:infectious** - Infectious encephalitis (viral, bacterial)
- **condition:nmda_receptor** - NMDA receptor encephalitis
- **condition:mog_ad** - MOG antibody-associated disease
- **condition:hsv** - Herpes simplex virus encephalitis
- **condition:tbe** - Tick-borne encephalitis

**Tip**: If they don't know the specific type yet, use general tags.

### 6. Where It Applies (Locations)

- **location:uk** - UK-specific (NHS, benefits, UK services)
- **location:worldwide** - Globally relevant
- **location:europe** - European-specific
- **location:usa**, **location:canada**, **location:australia**, etc.

**Tip**: Always check if content is UK-specific vs worldwide.

### 7. Resource Format

- **resource:factsheet** - Educational factsheet or guide
- **resource:video** - Video content
- **resource:research** - Research study, clinical trial
- **resource:news** - News article, blog post
- **resource:personal_story** - Patient or caregiver story
- **resource:event** - Conference, webinar, support group
- **resource:support_service** - Direct support (helpline, counseling)

**Tip**: Match format to caller's preference.

## Using Confidence Scores

Every classification has a **confidence score** (0-100%):

- **85-100%**: High confidence - Very reliable match
- **70-84%**: Medium confidence - Good match, spot check recommended
- **Below 70%**: Low confidence - Review before recommending

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Newly Diagnosed

**Caller**: "Just diagnosed, overwhelmed, need basics"

**Search for**:
- Personas: patient + caregiver
- Stage: pre_diagnosis
- Playlist: newly_diagnosed_pack

**Recommend**:
1. Newly Diagnosed Support Pack (playlist)
2. What is Encephalitis? (factsheet)
3. Understanding Your Diagnosis (video)

**Follow-up**: Email pack, schedule 2-week check-in

### Scenario 2: Specific Symptom

**Caller**: "Memory problems getting worse, need help"

**Search for**:
- Personas: patient
- Stage: early_recovery OR long_term_management
- Symptom: memory
- Topic: rehabilitation

**Recommend**:
1. Memory Problems in Encephalitis (factsheet)
2. Cognitive Rehabilitation Options (factsheet)
3. Memory Strategies and Tips (video)

**Follow-up**: Suggest occupational therapy referral

### Scenario 3: Caregiver Support

**Caller**: "I'm exhausted caring for my wife, need support"

**Search for**:
- Personas: caregiver
- Stage: long_term_management
- Playlist: caregiver_support

**Recommend**:
1. Caregiver Support Pack (playlist)
2. Looking After Yourself as a Carer (factsheet)
3. Caregiver Support Groups (event)

**Follow-up**: Connect with caregiver support coordinator

### Scenario 4: Professional Inquiry

**Caller**: "Neurologist, need treatment guidelines"

**Search for**:
- Personas: professional
- Type: autoimmune
- Topic: treatment
- Resource: research OR policy

**Recommend**:
1. Treatment Guidelines (policy)
2. Latest Research Updates (research)
3. Professional Education (webinar)

**Follow-up**: Add to professional mailing list

### Scenario 5: Bereaved Family

**Caller**: "Lost my son to encephalitis, need support"

**Search for**:
- Personas: bereaved
- Resource: support_service OR personal_story

**Recommend**:
1. Bereavement Support Services
2. Remembering Loved Ones (personal stories)
3. Bereaved Family Support Group

**Follow-up**: Sensitive handling, bereavement coordinator contact

## Best Practices

### Do's ✅

- **Match journey stage** - Don't overwhelm newly diagnosed with long-term content
- **Check confidence scores** - Higher is better
- **Use staff guidance** - It's there to help you
- **Combine resources** - Send a pack, not just one item
- **Follow up** - Schedule check-ins for ongoing support
- **Ask questions** - Clarify what they really need

### Don'ts ❌

- **Don't send everything** - Be selective and relevant
- **Don't ignore low confidence** - Review before recommending
- **Don't forget location** - UK vs worldwide matters
- **Don't skip follow-up** - People need ongoing support
- **Don't assume** - Ask about their specific situation

## Troubleshooting

### "I can't find what I need"

**Try**:
1. Broaden your search (remove some tags)
2. Try different tag combinations
3. Check if you're being too specific
4. Look at related resources
5. Ask a colleague or supervisor

### "Confidence score is low"

**What to do**:
1. Review the resource yourself
2. Check if it actually fits
3. Look for better alternatives
4. Flag for review if needed
5. Use your judgment

### "Person needs something not in system"

**Steps**:
1. Find closest available resource
2. Note the gap in your call log
3. Report to supervisor
4. System will learn and improve
5. Consider creating new resource

## Providing Feedback

### How to provide feedback

1. **Resource feedback**: "This resource was/wasn't helpful because..."
2. **Classification feedback**: "This tag should/shouldn't be applied because..."
3. **Gap identification**: "We need a resource for..."
4. **System feedback**: "The system could be improved by..."

### What happens with feedback

- Reviewed weekly by content team
- High-priority issues addressed immediately
- System improvements made monthly
- You'll be notified of changes
