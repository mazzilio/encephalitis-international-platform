## Understanding Tags (The Basics)

Every resource is tagged with simple categories:

### üë• Who Is It For?

- **Patient** - The person with encephalitis
- **Caregiver** - Family members, friends
- **Parent** - Parents of children with encephalitis
- **Professional** - Doctors, nurses, researchers
- **Bereaved** - Those who have lost someone

### üìÖ When Should You Use It?

- **Pre-diagnosis** - Before diagnosis, seeking help
- **Acute hospital** - During hospital stay
- **Early recovery** - First 6-12 months after hospital
- **Long-term** - Living with long-term effects (12+ months)

### üéØ What Does It Cover?

**Symptoms**: memory, behaviour, seizures, fatigue, mobility, speech, emotional

**Topics**: diagnosis, treatment, research, school, work, legal, travel

**Types**: factsheet, video, research, personal story, event, support service

### üåç Where Does It Apply?

- **UK** - UK-specific (NHS, benefits)
- **Worldwide** - Globally relevant
- **Country-specific** - USA, Canada, Australia, etc.

## Understanding Confidence Scores

Every resource has a confidence score (0-100%):

- **90-100%** üü¢ Excellent match - Recommend confidently
- **80-89%** üü¢ Very good match - Recommend
- **70-79%** üü° Good match - Quick check first
- **60-69%** üü° Fair match - Review carefully
- **Below 60%** üî¥ Poor match - Find alternative

**Example**: If a resource has 95% confidence for "newly diagnosed patient", you can be very confident it's the right resource.

## Common Questions

### "What if I can't find what I need?"

1. Try broader search terms
2. Remove some filters
3. Look at related resources
4. Note the gap - the system learns and improves
5. Ask your supervisor

### "What if the confidence score is low?"

1. Review the resource yourself
2. Check if it actually fits
3. Look for better alternatives
4. Use your professional judgment
5. Flag for review if needed

### "What if someone needs something not in the system?"

1. Find the closest available resource
2. Note the gap in your call log
3. Report to your supervisor
4. The system will learn and suggest adding it
5. Consider creating a new resource

### "How do I know the AI got it right?"

- Confidence scores show reliability
- Staff guidance is included for context
- You always make the final decision
- Low confidence items are flagged for review
- The system learns from feedback

## What Makes This Special?

### 1. It Learns

When the system encounters content that doesn't fit existing categories, it suggests new tags. For example:

- System processes 12 resources about visual hallucinations
- Realizes there's no specific tag for this
- Suggests adding "visual disturbances" tag
- After review, tag is added
- Future resources automatically get the right tag

### 2. It Gives You Guidance

Every resource includes practical tips:
- When to use it
- How to introduce it
- What tone to use
- Follow-up suggestions
- Sensitivity notes

### 3. It's Personalized

Resources are matched to:
- Who the person is (patient, caregiver, parent)
- Where they are in their journey (newly diagnosed, recovery)
- What they're experiencing (symptoms)
- Where they live (UK, worldwide)

### 4. It's Transparent

- Confidence scores show reliability
- You can see why resources were matched
- Low confidence items are flagged
- You always have the final say
